About The Ramayana Adventure RPG

The Ramayana Adventure is a narrative RPG adaptation of the Valmiki Ramayana designed for modern interactive web play. It combines lore-grounded storytelling with consequential gameplay systems, including combat development, affection arcs, personality tracking, inventory progression, and branching endings.

This project exists to present a foundational literary epic in an immersive format where players do not only read history—they shoulder its decisions. Every scene is written as a playable moral and strategic challenge.

Vision Statement

Our vision is to blend mythology and interactive design into a cohesive experience that is educational, emotionally resonant, and replayable. The game is built to respect the epic's philosophical core while using contemporary game architecture to make those ideas felt through action, consequence, and reflection.

Features Overview

  • Expanded scene-based RPG narrative from exile through Lanka War and Uttara Kanda.
  • Personality framework: Dharma, Aggression, Compassion, Honor, and Strategy.
  • Combat growth attributes: Strength, Defense, Speed, Agility, Stamina, and Endurance.
  • Character affection arcs with loyalty impact on battlefield and dialogue flow.
  • Day/night progression model with multi-scene daily cadence.
  • Save/export workflow and route-aware deep linking for campaign continuity.

Developer & Creator

The project is developed as a full-stack narrative systems portfolio work, combining front-end interaction design, scenario architecture, and scalable RPG state modeling. It is structured to continue growing into a production-level mythological strategy RPG with backend-assisted persistence and event processing.

About the Ramayana

The Ramayana is one of India's great Sanskrit epics, traditionally attributed to the sage Valmiki. It follows Prince Rama, Princess Sita, and Lakshmana through exile, loss, loyalty, and war against Ravana of Lanka.

Beyond its central conflict, the Ramayana explores themes of dharma (duty), honor, leadership, compassion, and the consequences of personal choices. Its stories have shaped literature, performing arts, and cultural traditions across South and Southeast Asia for centuries.
